important facts

Fractional RFÂ is the preferred skin tightening treatment

The delivery method of radiofrequency energy is through micro needles, which by themselves stimulate stem cell populations, and promote collagen and fibroblast production. However, the benefit of delivering radiofrequency in this method is that it bypasses the need to burn and heat the skin's surface entirely.

This is often an associated risk with Co2 ablative lasers as they excessively heat the surface in order to deliver energy to sub-dermal layers. Fractional RF trumps Co2 lasers in this regard as it delivers thermal energy directly inside the skin. This allows for a safer and more predictable treatment without the same burn risks, pain, and downtime.

Frequently asked questions

Will it burn the skin's surface?

The radiofrequency is tightly focused at the tip of each needle and the heat delivered is predominantly within the dermal tissue. This creates far less trauma to the skin's surface and less downtime compared with conventional Co2 fractional lasers.

Is fractional RF painful?

Even with the correct application of topical anaesthetic the treatment can be uncomfortable, but this depends on your skin condition and how deep or aggressive we need to be. It is possible to treat to a 1mm depth without requiring anaesthetic, however, this wouldn't be entirely pain-free either.

Can you treat over my beard?

Fractional RF impedes hair growth and will destroy hair follicles. For this reason we suggest that all male clients only book in for the half face treatment that excludes regions where facial hair normally grows. We can get very close to the edge of your beard with no risk of damaging the follicle. This also applies to areas around the hairline and eyebrows too, we only require a very small offset to ensure we don't damage any underlying hair follicles.

can you treat hooded eyelids?

We can treat above the eyes and below the brow by pushing the skin up into the brow bone. But this depends on your facial structure. If you have high brows with hooded eyelids there is often enough space for us to work in. Fractional RFÂ does impede hair growth so we do not treat this area if there isn't enough space to work with. If you have deep inset eyes and low brows then we cannot treat this area either. Skin needling may be a better solution in this area for you.

Skin needling or Fractional RF?

Skin needling and Fractional RF both rely on needles penetrating into the dermis. Skin needling is generally less painful as the needles are thinner and puncture the skin quickly. Whereas Fractional RF has larger needles that stay inside the skin to release radiofrequency. Although both treatments are considered tolerable, Fractional RF is more painful; with better overall clinical results.

How long is recovery?

You may experience transient erythema on the treated areas for up to 4 hours initially. It's possible that a thin scabbing will form on the surface (which is more akin to a dark, dry tan), however, do not be concerned by this as this promotes the healing process.

How many treatments do I need?

This depends on the area you are trying to treat and the condition of your skin. The typical interval between treatments is normally every 3 weeks. Most patients see excellent results after 3 treatments. This may vary according to your skin condition, or the depth of wrinkles and scarring. We will be able to discuss this in greater detail when we assess your skin in person.

Preparation
Aftercare

  • Do not apply any makeup, lotions, spray tans or creams prior to your appointment

  • Let us know if you have a nickel (metal) allergy

  • Avoid any sun exposure

  • 3 days prior to your treatment, do not use any salicylic acid, glycolic acid or Retin-AÂ® which may irritate your skin

  • After your treatment your skin may feel warm, tingly, itchy or slightly sensitive for up to 48 hours

  • Avoid cleansing the treated area on the day of the treatment. Start on Day 2

  • Cleanse the skin with cold water and dab or pat lightly with a towel
